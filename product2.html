About Analog Chips

Analog chips, also referred to as analog integrated circuits (ICs), are essential components in electronic systems that deal with continuous signals. Unlike digital chips that process discrete binary data, analog chips handle signals that vary smoothly over time, such as voltage, current, or frequency. These chips perform various functions related to signal processing, including amplification, filtering, modulation, and demodulation. They are crucial for interfacing with the physical world, as they interact with sensors to acquire real-world data and convert it into usable information for electronic devices. Analog chips are widely used in applications such as audio processing, sensor interfaces, power management, and communication systems. They play a vital role in devices ranging from smartphones and consumer electronics to industrial automation and medical equipment.
